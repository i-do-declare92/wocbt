    • For thousands of years, people of many cultures believed that life was the product of an Intelligent Designer. How else, they would reason, could the vast array of lifeforms have come into existence?

    Some thought that the answer to that question had been found when Charles Darwin published his seminal work, On the Origin of Species. Many modern scientists felt this finally removed the need of God as an explanation for the diversity we see in life. Perhaps, even, for the very origin of life itself.

    160 years later, what has research in the fields of genetics and paleontology shown? Did all life today descend from a universal common ancestor? Or is there historical evidence of special creation? Find out as we tour the “Evolving Planet” exhibit at the Chicago Field Museum and examine what the fossil record has to say.
