    For centuries, people have been digging up the fossilized remains of ancient creatures. Long has our collective imagination been captured by these glimpses into the past. But no other group of animals—living or extinct—has fascinated us like the dinosaurs. We erect their massive skeletons in our museums, they inspire Hollywood blockbusters, and countless television documentaries, books, video games, and toys.

    But what exactly is a dinosaur (and what is not)? When did they live, and why were they created? When did they die off, and how? And how exactly do paleontologists learn about ancient worlds and life that have been extinct long before any human ever walked the Earth?

    Join us as we explore the most famous inhabitants of the Mesozoic Era, the dinosaurs, and their 160 million-year reign of dominance over the land. We will explore dinosaurs museum exhibits from around the world as we delve into the exciting world of paleontology and build our appreciation for these masterpieces of the Grand Creator.
